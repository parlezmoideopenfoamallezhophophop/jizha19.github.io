About Me

Jian Zhang https://orcid.org/0000-0003-4913-2584 is a talented postdoctoral researcher at University Laval with a strong interest in the field of polymer rheology, mechanical fatigue of biobased/recycled polymers, and fluid dynamics in porous media. Throughout his academic and professional career, Jian has demonstrated a passion for understanding the fundamental behavior of complex materials and has made significant contributions to the field through his research. He has extensive experience in characterizing the mechanical and rheological properties of polymers, as well as investigating the effects of mechanical fatigue on these materials. Additionally, Jian has a keen interest in the fluid dynamics of porous media and has conducted research on the behavior of fluids in these complex environments. With a commitment to excellence in research and a dedication to advancing the field of polymer science, Jian is a valuable asset to any team or project.

In addition to his impressive research background, Jian Zhang has also achieved notable academic accomplishments, including eight publications in peer-reviewed journals that showcase his ability to communicate complex research findings to a broad audience. His contributions to the field have been recognized with several prestigious awards, including the Mitacs award, which highlights his exceptional research skills and expertise. Jian has also received a PhD scholarship, which demonstrates his commitment to academic excellence and his potential to make significant contributions to the field of polymer science. Furthermore, Jian has participated in a summer school program at Ghent University, where he expanded his knowledge and expertise in the field of polymer science, and developed a global perspective on the latest research advancements. With such an outstanding track record of academic achievement, Jian is well-equipped to continue making significant contributions to the field of polymer rheology and beyond.
